# Prompt Templates

18 ready-to-paste templates organized by category. Copy any template into Claude.ai and fill in the `[BRACKETED PLACEHOLDERS]`.

---

## Outbound Sales

### 1. Cold Outreach Email
```
Write a cold outreach email.

Sender: [YOUR NAME], [YOUR TITLE] at [YOUR COMPANY]
Recipient: [THEIR TITLE] at a [COMPANY SIZE] [INDUSTRY] company
Goal: [DESIRED ACTION — e.g., book a 15-minute introductory call]
Our value prop: [ONE SENTENCE]
Proof point: [STAT OR CUSTOMER RESULT]

Tone: professional but human
Length: under 100 words
Open with their problem, not our product.
End with a low-commitment question.
Do not use: "just reaching out," "I hope this finds you well"
```
*Use for: First touch to a cold prospect.*

### 2. Follow-Up Email (No Response)
```
I sent this email [NUMBER] days ago and got no response:
[PASTE YOUR ORIGINAL EMAIL]

Write a follow-up that:
- References the original without repeating it
- Adds a new angle or piece of value
- Is shorter than the original
- Does not say "just following up" or "checking in"
- Ends with a yes/no question to make replying easy
```
*Use for: Second or third touch when a prospect hasn't replied.*

### 3. Objection Handling Email
```
A prospect raised this objection: "[EXACT OBJECTION — e.g., 'We're already
using ProjectBeam and don't want to switch tools right now.']"

Write a reply email that:
- Acknowledges their concern without being dismissive
- Addresses the objection with [SPECIFIC COUNTER-POINT OR PROOF]
- Offers a low-commitment next step (not a hard sell)
- Tone: empathetic and consultative
- Length: under 120 words
```
*Use for: Responding to specific sales objections via email.*

### 4. Meeting Request Email
```
Write an email requesting a meeting.

Context: [WHY YOU WANT TO MEET — what you'll discuss and why it's valuable to them]
Recipient: [THEIR ROLE AND COMPANY TYPE]
Proposed format: [15-min call / 30-min demo / lunch meeting]
Urgency: [TIME-SENSITIVE REASON OR "no specific deadline"]

Tone: respectful of their time, confident in the value
Length: under 100 words
Include 2-3 suggested time slots or a calendar link CTA.
```
*Use for: Requesting introductory meetings or demos.*

## Marketing Content

### 5. Blog Post
```
Write a blog post about [TOPIC].

Target audience: [WHO — role, industry, level of expertise]
Goal: [Drive traffic / educate / generate leads / build authority]
Key points to cover:
- [POINT 1]
- [POINT 2]
- [POINT 3]

Length: [WORD COUNT] words
Tone: [TONE]
Include: specific examples, data where possible, a clear CTA at the end
SEO keywords to include naturally: [KEYWORD 1], [KEYWORD 2]
Structure: intro hook, [N] main sections with subheadings, conclusion with CTA
```
*Use for: Creating blog content from scratch.*

### 6. Social Media Post
```
Write a [PLATFORM — LinkedIn / Twitter / Instagram] post about [TOPIC].

Context: [WHAT HAPPENED, WHAT YOU'RE ANNOUNCING, OR WHAT POINT YOU'RE MAKING]
Tone: [TONE — conversational / professional / bold / thoughtful]
Length: [WORD COUNT OR CHARACTER LIMIT]
Include: [SPECIFIC ELEMENTS — stat, question, CTA, hashtags (max 3)]
Avoid: [WHAT NOT TO DO — generic phrases, buzzwords, excessive hashtags]
```
*Use for: Creating individual social posts for any platform.*

### 7. Case Study
```
Write a case study using this structure:

Customer: [COMPANY NAME, SIZE, INDUSTRY]
Situation: [THEIR CONTEXT BEFORE USING YOUR PRODUCT]
Challenge: [SPECIFIC PROBLEM THEY WERE FACING]
Solution: [HOW THEY USED YOUR PRODUCT]
Result: [QUANTIFIED OUTCOMES — numbers, percentages, time saved]

Include:
- A headline that leads with the result (e.g., "[Company] Reduced [Problem] by [X]%")
- A 2-sentence executive summary at the top
- A realistic pull quote from the customer
- Specific numbers throughout

Length: [WORD COUNT] words
Tone: professional but story-driven
```
*Use for: Writing customer success stories.*

### 8. Press Release
```
Write a press release announcing [NEWS].

Company: [YOUR COMPANY NAME AND BRIEF DESCRIPTION]
News: [WHAT YOU'RE ANNOUNCING]
Why it matters: [SIGNIFICANCE TO THE MARKET OR CUSTOMERS]
Quote from: [NAME, TITLE] — focus the quote on [KEY MESSAGE]
Availability: [WHEN AND HOW PEOPLE CAN ACCESS/USE THIS]
Boilerplate: [YOUR COMPANY DESCRIPTION — 2-3 sentences]
Contact: [NAME, EMAIL]

Format: Standard press release format with headline, dateline, body, boilerplate
Length: 400-500 words
Tone: newsworthy and factual, not promotional
```
*Use for: Drafting press releases for product launches, partnerships, or milestones.*

## Research & Analysis

### 9. Competitor Analysis
```
Analyze this competitor based on the following materials:

Competitor: [NAME]
Their materials: [PASTE WEBSITE COPY, PRODUCT DESCRIPTIONS, OR UPLOAD PDF]

Our company: [YOUR COMPANY]
Our differentiators: [LIST 3-5]

Produce:
1. Their positioning summary (3 sentences)
2. Their target audience (inferred from their copy)
3. Messaging strengths and weaknesses
4. 3 counter-positioning statements we can use
```
*Use for: Quick competitive messaging analysis.*

### 10. Market Summary
```
Based on the following information, write a market summary on [TOPIC]:

[PASTE ARTICLES, DATA, OR NOTES YOU'VE COLLECTED]

Target audience for this summary: [WHO WILL READ THIS — e.g., marketing leadership]
Scope: [WHAT ASPECTS TO COVER — trends, opportunities, threats, sizing]
Depth: [EXECUTIVE OVERVIEW / DETAILED ANALYSIS]
Length: [WORD COUNT] words
Include: key takeaways, implications for our business, recommended actions
```
*Use for: Synthesizing research into an actionable summary.*

### 11. Data Interpretation
```
Here is our data:
[PASTE TABLE OR UPLOAD CSV]

Analyze this data and provide:
1. Executive summary (3 sentences)
2. Top [N] trends or patterns
3. Areas of concern with suggested next steps
4. [N] talking points for a [AUDIENCE] presentation
```
*Use for: Turning numbers into narrative and talking points.*

### 12. Customer Persona Builder
```
Help me build a customer persona based on what we know:

Demographics: [AGE RANGE, ROLE, INDUSTRY, COMPANY SIZE]
Pain points: [WHAT FRUSTRATES THEM — list 3-5]
Goals: [WHAT THEY'RE TRYING TO ACHIEVE]
Common objections: [WHAT THEY SAY WHEN THEY DON'T BUY]
Where they get information: [CHANNELS, PUBLICATIONS, COMMUNITIES]
Current tools they use: [COMPETING OR ADJACENT PRODUCTS]

Create a complete persona document with:
- Name and title (realistic)
- Day-in-the-life description (one paragraph)
- Motivations and frustrations
- How our product helps them
- Messaging themes that would resonate
```
*Use for: Creating or refining buyer personas.*

## Editing & Polishing

### 13. Tone Adjuster
```
Rewrite this text in a [TARGET TONE — e.g., casual, formal, urgent,
empathetic, authoritative] tone.

Keep the same core message and structure. Only change how it sounds.

Constraints: [ANY SPECIFIC RULES — e.g., "keep under 100 words,"
"maintain all data points"]

Text to rewrite:
[PASTE TEXT]
```
*Use for: Adapting content for different channels or audiences.*

### 14. Length Reducer
```
Cut this text to under [TARGET WORD COUNT] words.

Preserve: [WHAT MUST STAY — key message, CTA, specific data points]
Remove: anything that doesn't directly support the main point

Text to shorten:
[PASTE TEXT]
```
*Use for: Tightening copy that's too long.*

### 15. Clarity Improver
```
Edit this text for clarity. Target audience: [WHO — their expertise level].

Specifically:
- Simplify complex sentences
- Replace jargon with plain language
- Make every sentence understandable on first read
- [ADDITIONAL RULES — e.g., "no acronyms without expansion"]

Text to improve:
[PASTE TEXT]
```
*Use for: Making technical or dense copy more accessible.*

## Strategic

### 16. Campaign Brief Generator
```
Generate a campaign brief for [CAMPAIGN NAME OR TOPIC].

Goals: [WHAT SUCCESS LOOKS LIKE — metrics and outcomes]
Target audience: [WHO]
Channels: [WHERE — email, social, paid, webinar, etc.]
Timeline: [START AND END DATES]
Budget: [AMOUNT OR "TBD"]
Key messages: [2-3 CORE MESSAGES OR "suggest based on the goal"]

Include sections: objective, audience, messaging, channel plan with
tactics, timeline with milestones, success metrics, budget allocation
```
*Use for: Quickly generating campaign brief first drafts.*

### 17. Positioning Statement
```
Write a positioning statement using this framework:

For [TARGET CUSTOMER — who they are]
Who [NEED/PROBLEM — what they struggle with]
[PRODUCT NAME] is a [CATEGORY — what type of product it is]
That [KEY BENEFIT — what it does for them]
Unlike [COMPETITORS OR ALTERNATIVES]
Our product [KEY DIFFERENTIATOR — what makes us unique]

Then expand this into a 2-3 sentence positioning paragraph suitable for
an internal strategy document.
```
*Use for: Crafting or refining product positioning.*

### 18. SWOT Analysis
```
Create a SWOT analysis based on the following:

Company/product: [NAME]
Context: [PASTE RELEVANT BACKGROUND — market position, recent developments, competitive landscape]

Focus areas: [SPECIFIC ASPECTS TO EVALUATE — e.g., messaging, market position, product capabilities]

Format as a 2x2 table with 3-5 items per quadrant.
Add a "Key Takeaway" sentence after the table summarizing the most
important strategic implication.
```
*Use for: Structured strategic analysis of your company or competitors.*
