# Week 2 Challenges

Optional reinforcement exercises for the week after completing the course. One challenge per day, each building on what you learned.

---

## Monday — Real-World Reps (Day 1 Reinforcement)

**Challenge:** Use Claude for 3 real work tasks today — not practice exercises, but actual work you need to get done.

**Suggestions:**
- Draft an email you need to send
- Summarize notes from a meeting
- Rewrite a piece of internal copy

**Success criteria:**
- [ ] You used Claude for 3 separate real tasks
- [ ] At least one task saved you meaningful time
- [ ] You noted what worked and what you'd do differently

**Estimated time:** 15-20 minutes (spread across the day)

---

## Tuesday — Specificity Challenge (Day 2 Reinforcement)

**Challenge:** Take your most-used type of request (e.g., "write a follow-up email") and write it at all three levels of the Specificity Ladder.

**Steps:**
1. Write a Level 1 (vague) version and send it to Claude
2. Write a Level 2 (directed) version and send it
3. Write a Level 3 (fully specified) version and send it
4. Save the Level 3 version as a reusable template

**Success criteria:**
- [ ] You have three versions of the same request
- [ ] The quality difference between Level 1 and Level 3 is obvious
- [ ] You saved the Level 3 version for future use

**Estimated time:** 15 minutes

---

## Wednesday — Publish Something (Day 3 Reinforcement)

**Challenge:** Produce a complete, publish-ready piece of content using the Draft-Critique-Revise loop.

**Steps:**
1. Choose a real content need (blog post, email sequence, social post, or case study)
2. Draft it using the techniques from Day 3
3. Run the full Draft-Critique-Revise loop
4. Edit the final version yourself and publish or share it

**Success criteria:**
- [ ] You completed the full Draft-Critique-Revise loop
- [ ] The final piece required only minor personal editing
- [ ] You actually published or shared the piece (email sent, post published, doc shared)

**Estimated time:** 20-25 minutes

---

## Thursday — Deliver a Brief (Day 4 Reinforcement)

**Challenge:** Build a real competitive intelligence brief or research summary and share it with your manager or team.

**Steps:**
1. Choose a competitor or research topic relevant to current work
2. Gather the raw materials (paste in competitor website copy, data, or articles)
3. Use the Competitive Intelligence Brief template or the Brief Builder approach
4. Review and edit the output
5. Share it with your manager or team

**Success criteria:**
- [ ] The brief covers a real competitor or topic, not a practice one
- [ ] You used actual source materials (not just Claude's general knowledge)
- [ ] Someone on your team found the brief useful

**Estimated time:** 25-30 minutes

---

## Friday — Team Infrastructure (Day 5 Reinforcement)

**Challenge:** Set up a shared team Project and onboard at least one colleague.

**Steps:**
1. Create a Project for your team's most common use case (or refine the one you built in Day 5)
2. Write thorough Custom Instructions
3. Upload 2-3 Knowledge Files
4. Test it with 3 different types of requests
5. Share it with at least one teammate and walk them through how to use it

**Success criteria:**
- [ ] The Project is set up with instructions and knowledge files
- [ ] It produces quality output across different task types without extra context
- [ ] At least one teammate has access and has tried it

**Estimated time:** 20-25 minutes

---

## What's Next?

After Week 2, you should have:
- A personal habit of using Claude for daily tasks
- Reusable prompt templates saved for your most common work
- At least one shared team Project
- A clear sense of where Claude saves you the most time

Keep building on this foundation:
- Add new prompts to your team's Prompt Library as you discover them
- Experiment with file uploads and integrations for more complex tasks
- Share what's working with teammates — the best teams learn from each other
