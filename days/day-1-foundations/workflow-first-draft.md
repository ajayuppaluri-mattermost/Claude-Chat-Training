# Workflow: First Draft in 5 Minutes

Copy this template into Claude.ai whenever you need a first draft of any document. Fill in the bracketed placeholders with your specifics.

## The Template

```
I need you to write a [DOCUMENT TYPE] for [AUDIENCE].

Context:
- [KEY POINT 1]
- [KEY POINT 2]
- [KEY POINT 3]

Requirements:
- Tone: [formal / conversational / urgent / friendly / authoritative]
- Length: [word count or "short" / "medium" / "long"]
- Must include: [SPECIFIC ELEMENTS — e.g., a call-to-action, specific data points, a deadline]
- Must avoid: [THINGS TO EXCLUDE — e.g., jargon, competitor mentions, pricing]

Please produce a complete first draft.
```

## Filled Examples

**Example 1: Sales Follow-Up Email**
```
I need you to write a follow-up email for a prospect who attended our webinar but didn't book a demo.

Context:
- Webinar topic: "5 Ways AI Saves Marketing Teams 10 Hours Per Week"
- The prospect is a marketing director at a mid-size e-commerce company
- Our product automates report generation and campaign analysis
- Webinar was 3 days ago

Requirements:
- Tone: helpful and low-pressure, not salesy
- Length: under 150 words
- Must include: one specific insight from the webinar, a soft CTA to book a 15-minute call
- Must avoid: aggressive language, multiple CTAs, "just following up" opener

Please produce a complete first draft.
```

**Example 2: Internal Announcement**
```
I need you to write an internal announcement email for all employees.

Context:
- We're launching a new AI-powered feature in our product next month
- The sales team needs to start learning the talking points now
- Training sessions will be held in weeks 2 and 3 of March
- Product marketing has a new one-pager available on the intranet

Requirements:
- Tone: excited but professional
- Length: under 200 words
- Must include: what the feature does (in plain language), why it matters for sales, dates for training, where to find the one-pager
- Must avoid: technical jargon, anything that sounds like it's optional

Please produce a complete first draft.
```

**Example 3: LinkedIn Post**
```
I need you to write a LinkedIn post for our company page.

Context:
- We just published a case study about how CustomerCo reduced churn by 25% using our platform
- The case study link is: [URL]
- Key stat: they saw results within 60 days of implementation

Requirements:
- Tone: conversational and confident, not braggy
- Length: 150-200 words
- Must include: the 25% stat, a quote-style sentence from the customer, the link, and end with a question to drive engagement
- Must avoid: hashtag spam (max 3 hashtags), generic phrases like "excited to share"

Please produce a complete first draft.
```

## Tips

- **Start with this template every time.** Even if you only fill in half the fields, it's better than a blank prompt.
- **After the first draft, refine.** Say "Make it shorter," "Change the tone to X," or "Add a section about Y."
- **Save your best versions.** When a prompt works well, save it somewhere you can reuse it. You'll build a personal prompt library over the course of this week.
