# Day 3 Exercises

Today's exercises use realistic marketing scenarios. You'll practice the Draft-Critique-Revise loop, multi-piece content creation, and tone control.

---

## Exercise 1: The Campaign Email Suite (10 min)

**Scenario:** Your company is launching "SmartReport," an AI feature that auto-generates weekly project status reports. You need a 3-email nurture sequence targeting marketing operations managers.

**Steps:**

1. Open a new chat in Claude.ai
2. Paste this prompt to get all three emails at once:

```
You are a B2B email marketing specialist.

Write a 3-email nurture sequence for the launch of SmartReport, an AI
feature that automatically generates weekly project status reports and
emails them to stakeholders every Monday morning.

Target audience: Marketing operations managers at companies with 200-1000 employees.
Pain point: They spend 3-4 hours every week manually compiling status updates
from multiple tools (Asana, Jira, Salesforce) into a report for leadership.

Email 1 — Awareness (sent launch day):
- Subject line + body
- Focus on the pain point, introduce the solution
- CTA: Watch a 2-minute demo video

Email 2 — Consideration (sent 3 days later):
- Subject line + body
- Focus on a specific customer result: "DataCorp reduced reporting time from 4 hours to 10 minutes"
- CTA: Start a free trial

Email 3 — Decision (sent 5 days after Email 2):
- Subject line + body
- Focus on ease of setup (connects in 5 minutes, no IT required) and limited-time offer (60-day trial vs. standard 14-day)
- CTA: Start extended free trial

For all emails:
- Tone: helpful and conversational, not salesy
- Length: 100-150 words each
- Short paragraphs (2-3 sentences max)
- One CTA per email
```

3. Read all three emails
4. Now apply the Draft-Critique-Revise loop:

```
Review these three emails as a senior email marketing manager.
For each email, identify:
- Is the subject line compelling enough to open?
- Does the opening line hook the reader immediately?
- Is the CTA clear and well-positioned?
- Is the messaging consistent across all three?

Be specific about what needs to change.
```

5. Then:

```
Revise all three emails based on your critique.
```

**What good looks like:** Three emails that feel like a coherent sequence — each builds on the last, the messaging is consistent, the urgency escalates naturally, and each one could stand alone. Subject lines should be specific and curiosity-driven.

## Exercise 2: Blog Post From Scratch (12 min)

**Scenario:** You need a blog post that drives organic traffic and positions your company as a thought leader.

**Steps:**

1. Open a new chat in Claude.ai
2. Get headline options:

```
Give me 5 headline options for a blog post about how marketing teams
are using AI to automate their weekly reporting.

Target audience: Marketing managers and directors at mid-size B2B companies.
Goal: Drive organic search traffic and demonstrate expertise.

Make them specific (include numbers or outcomes where possible).
Avoid generic AI hype — focus on the practical workflow change.
```

3. Pick the best headline (or tell Claude which number you like). Then get an outline:

```
I'm going with headline #[YOUR CHOICE].

Create a blog post outline with:
- An opening hook that describes a relatable frustration (not a generic AI statement)
- 4 main sections with descriptive subheadings
- Key points for each section
- A conclusion with a CTA to try our product
- Target: 800 words total
```

4. Draft one section:

```
Write the first section (after the intro). Target 200 words.
Include a specific, concrete example — not generic advice.
```

5. Now run the critique-revise loop on just that section:

```
Critique this section: Is it specific enough? Does it provide actionable
value or is it too high-level? Would a marketing manager learn something
new from reading it?
```

Then:

```
Revise the section based on your critique. Make it more specific and actionable.
```

**What good looks like:** A headline that you'd actually click on, an outline that flows logically, and a drafted section that provides real value — not filler content. The revised section should be noticeably better than the first draft.

## Exercise 3: Tone Transformer (5 min)

**Scenario:** You have one paragraph of product copy and need to adapt it for three different contexts.

**Steps:**

1. Open a new chat in Claude.ai
2. Paste this prompt:

```
Here is a paragraph from our product page:

"TaskFlow Pro uses machine learning to analyze your team's workload,
project timelines, and resource availability in real time. When it detects
a potential bottleneck — like an overloaded team member or a slipping
deadline — it alerts you with specific recommendations before the problem
affects your project timeline. The result: fewer surprises, fewer fire
drills, and projects that finish on schedule."

Rewrite this paragraph in three different tones. Keep the core message
the same, but change the voice:

Version A: Casual and conversational (for a LinkedIn post)
Version B: Authoritative and data-driven (for a whitepaper)
Version C: Urgent and action-oriented (for a promotional email)

Label each version clearly.
```

3. Compare the three versions. They should say the same thing but feel completely different.

**What good looks like:** Three versions that are clearly distinct in voice. The LinkedIn version should feel like a person talking. The whitepaper version should feel substantive and credible. The promotional email should create urgency without being desperate.
