# Day 4 Exercises

These exercises include sample data and text so you can practice immediately. Use the provided materials or substitute your own real-world data.

## Exercise 1: Competitor Teardown (10 min)

**Scenario:** Your leadership team wants a competitive messaging analysis before next quarter's campaign planning. You need to quickly analyze a competitor's positioning.

**Steps:**

1. Open a new chat in Claude.ai
2. Paste this sample competitor copy (or substitute copy from a real competitor's website):

```
Analyze this competitor's marketing copy. The competitor is "ProjectBeam,"
a project management platform.

Their homepage hero copy:
"ProjectBeam: Where Teams Do Their Best Work. Trusted by 50,000+ teams
worldwide, ProjectBeam brings your projects, tasks, and team communication
into one powerful platform. From startups to Fortune 500, teams use
ProjectBeam to ship faster, stay aligned, and never miss a deadline."

Their product page:
"Everything your team needs in one place. ProjectBeam combines task
management, real-time collaboration, Gantt charts, time tracking, and
AI-powered workflows into a single platform. No more switching between
tools. No more lost context. Just pure productivity.

Features: Kanban boards, Gantt timelines, resource management, custom
dashboards, 200+ integrations, mobile app, enterprise-grade security.

Starting at $12/user/month."

Their 'Why ProjectBeam' page:
"Teams switch to ProjectBeam because they're tired of tool sprawl.
The average team uses 6 different apps to manage one project. ProjectBeam
replaces them all. Our customers report 35% fewer meetings and 2x faster
project completion after switching."

Based on this copy, provide:
1. Their positioning summary (3 sentences: who they target, what they claim, how they differentiate)
2. Their target audience (be specific about company size, role, pain points)
3. Top 3 messaging strengths (what they do well in their copy)
4. Top 3 messaging weaknesses (where their copy is vague, generic, or unconvincing)
5. 3 counter-positioning statements we could use (assuming our differentiator is AI-powered resource allocation that predicts and prevents project delays)
```

3. Review the analysis
4. Ask a follow-up:

```
Now draft a one-paragraph competitive positioning statement we could use
internally. It should explain: who ProjectBeam is, where they're strong,
where they're weak, and how we win against them.
```

**What good looks like:** An analysis that goes beyond surface observations. The strengths should identify specific effective copy techniques. The weaknesses should point to real gaps (e.g., "AI-powered workflows" is vague — what do they actually automate?). Counter-positioning should be specific to your actual differentiator.

## Exercise 2: Quarterly Data Storyteller (8 min)

**Scenario:** You need to prepare talking points for a leadership meeting using last quarter's marketing data.

**Steps:**

1. Open a new chat in Claude.ai
2. Paste this data table:

```
Here is our marketing performance data for the past 4 quarters:

| Metric | Q1 2025 | Q2 2025 | Q3 2025 | Q4 2025 |
|--------|---------|---------|---------|---------|
| Website Visits | 45,000 | 52,000 | 48,000 | 67,000 |
| MQLs | 450 | 520 | 480 | 610 |
| SQLs | 180 | 195 | 160 | 245 |
| MQL→SQL Rate | 40% | 37.5% | 33.3% | 40.2% |
| Closed Deals | 45 | 52 | 38 | 61 |
| Revenue | $890K | $1.1M | $950K | $1.4M |
| CAC | $285 | $310 | $340 | $295 |
| Email List Size | 12,000 | 13,500 | 14,200 | 16,800 |
| Email Open Rate | 28% | 25% | 23% | 31% |
| LinkedIn Followers | 8,200 | 9,100 | 9,800 | 12,400 |

Analyze this data and provide:
1. A 3-sentence executive summary of the full year
2. The top 3 positive trends
3. The 2 biggest areas of concern
4. 5 talking points for a board presentation (each one sentence, lead with the insight not the number)
```

3. Read the analysis. Then ask:

```
What are the 5 most likely questions our CEO would ask when seeing this data?
For each question, draft a concise answer backed by the numbers.
```

4. Finally:

```
Q3 was clearly a dip. Write a brief explanation (3-4 sentences) of what
likely happened in Q3 and why Q4 represents a strong recovery. Frame it
honestly but positively.
```

**What good looks like:** An analysis that spots patterns (Q3 dip and Q4 recovery, improving CAC trend, LinkedIn growth acceleration), not just restates numbers. Talking points that lead with business implications. CEO questions that reflect real executive concerns.

## Exercise 3: Industry Brief Builder (8 min)

**Scenario:** You need a quick research brief on a trending topic for your next team meeting.

**Steps:**

1. Open a new chat in Claude.ai
2. Start with the structure:

```
I need to create a 1-page research brief on "AI adoption in B2B
marketing operations" for our marketing leadership team.

What should a thorough brief cover? Give me a recommended outline
with a description of what each section should contain.
Keep it focused — this is a 1-page brief, not a whitepaper.
```

3. Pick the outline Claude suggests (or adjust it). Then draft the key section:

```
Write the executive summary section (150 words max).

What we know:
- Our company is a B2B SaaS project management tool
- Our target market is mid-size companies (200-1000 employees)
- We recently added AI features (smart reporting, resource prediction)
- Our competitors are all adding AI features too
- Our customers frequently ask about AI capabilities in demos

Make this actionable for a marketing leader deciding how to position
AI in our Q2 messaging.
```

4. Then identify gaps:

```
What important questions does this brief NOT yet answer?
What data or research would make it more actionable?
List 5 specific gaps with suggestions for how to fill them.
```

**What good looks like:** A well-structured outline that a non-expert could follow. An executive summary that's specific to your company's situation (not generic AI commentary). Gap analysis that identifies genuinely useful next steps.
